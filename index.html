Po Chuan Chen

Hello! I am Po Chuan Chen (陳博詮), a passionate exoskeleton maker with a strong background in control and real-time systems. I am worknig in Dr. Aaron Young's lab (Exoskeleton and Prosthetic Intelligent Controls (EPIC) Lab) at Georgia Institute of Technology where I received my MS in Electrical and Computer Enginneering, specializing in control. In the lab, I focus on developing innovative exoskeleton controllers that can adapt effectively to diverse walking conditions. I am driven by the goal of creating robust solutions that enhance the functionality of exoskeletons.

Before I came to Georgia Tech, I worked for 3.5 years as an embedded software engineer in the television department at Mediatek. During my time there, my main focus was on the development of digital image processing algorithms and addressing real-time system concerns. In addition to my industrial experience, I have also delved into various academic research areas spanning a wide range of fields. These include studying liquid crystal lenses for 3D displays, music genre classification, natural language processing, and controller for exoskeletons.

Research

My primary interest centers on the intersection of Biomechanics, Adaptive/Optimal Control, and Machine Learning. Project

Hip Exoskeleton
Develop novel exoskeleton controllers allowing robust adaptation to various walking conditions. The controllers are based on the electromyography-driven neuromuscular model to simulate muscle activities and biological torque for a high-torque hip exoskeleton. The primary purpose of this research is to reduce metabolic costs for older people at their self-select-walking speed.

Hand Exoskeleton
Built an exoskeleton‑based rehabilitation system from scratch, including mechanical structure, control system, and electronic design, for patients with peripheral nerve injuries. The system was controlled via electromyography (EMG) of the patient and was operated on an Arduino embedded development board.
